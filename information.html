What:

Period Poverty is the lack of access to sanitary feminine hygiene products, menstrual hygiene education, and/or waste management. In the US, 35 states impose a sales tax known as the “tampon tax,” on menstrual hygiene products. Period products cannot be purchased with food stamps, Medicaid, or other governement aid benefits.

Statistics: Two-thirds of the women surveyed in a 2019 study did not have the resources to buy menstrual hygiene products within the last year while one-fifth of women struggled to afford period products on a monthly basis

Why:

#end.periodpoverty is taking the initiative to raise awareness to the inadequate access many girls and women have to sanitary products and menstrual education. We also aim to educate disadvantaged individuals about periods. According to UNICEF poor menstrual hygiene can increase the likelihood of physical health risks and contracting reproductive and urinary tract infections. When girls and women miss out on opportunities crucial to their growth, it stop them from reaching their full potentials.

Statistics: In several countries around the world, 2 of 3 girls may miss school while menstruating due to lack of period education and access in addition to cultural or social stigma.

Who:

Our primary audience include low income, special needs/disabilities, and homeless. These impacted group of girls disproportionately do not have access to the facilities and resources they need for proper menstrual hygiene. Additionally, residing in areas riddled with conflict or natural disasters also makes it increasingly difficult for women and girls to manage their periods properly.

Statistics: 1-in-5 girls miss school every year due to not having adequate menstrual products.
